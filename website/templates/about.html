About

Welcome

I built this project to answer the most burning questions a younger version of myself had when I first got interested in markets, trading, and technical analysis many years ago – what’s the best technical strategy and how do I know it works? Before knowing anything about coding or basic backtesting methods, the only assurance I had that a particular trading strategy worked - out of hundreds of different strategies and tens of thousands of possible combinations of parameter inputs – was either the misguided/self-interested testimonial of a “guru” or by manually backtesting a single version of a single strategy over X periods of Y timeframe on Z asset. As one can imagine, this turned out to be incredibly time consuming, tedious, and inefficient. Since ain't nobody got time for that, the obvious answer was the let the computer do it.

I started with developing a single backtest for an easy technical based strategy. The concept was simple - input a handful of parameters and receive a list of trades taken as well as a few evaluation metrics to gauge performance. Once finished, I excitedly started inputting random parameter combinations and viewing the results. A few minutes later I hit the "ok, what now?" wall and came to the realization that this really didn't get me much closer to finding anything substantial. After all, there are thousands, if not millions of combinations of parameter inputs for any given strategy. How would I sort through them all to find the performers? Once again, I defaulted to the obvious answer - let the computer do it.

The result is what you see before you. If you're curious about how it works and what goes into it you can find the answer here.
